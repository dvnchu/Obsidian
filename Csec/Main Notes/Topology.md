[[Redes]]
### Star Topology
![[Pasted image 20251215184143.png]]

Devices connected via a central networking device (switch/hub), Reliable and scalable but kinda costly.

Any info sent to a device in this topology is sent via the central device.

### Bus Topology
![[Pasted image 20251215185033.png]]
Connection with a single backbone or Bus cable, data travels along this cable, it has problems with slowness and possible bottlenecks; It is also very difficult to troubleshoot as it is difficult to identify the device with the issue (Also if the backbone cable breaks devices can no longer receive or transmit data).

However it is one of the easier and more cost efficient topologies to setup.


### Ring Topology
![[Pasted image 20251215190843.png]]
Devices connected to each other forming a loop. little cabling and less dependence on hardware like switches
If the actual device have data to send, it will send its own data first and then the data from another device, they form a loop.

fairly easy to troubleshoot as it is unidirectional, but data may have to travel across multiple devices.

not so prone to bottlenecks as data is not traveling across the network at any one time.
