Both of these are small pieces of data that, when forming together make a larger piece of information of message 
##### Packet
Piece of data from layer 3 ([[OSI Model#2.Data Link|Data Link]]), contains info such as an IP header and a payload
##### Frame
Used at layer 2 ([[OSI Model#3.Network|Network]]), encapsulates the packet and adds additional info such as mac addresses

This process is called encapsulation.

Packets are an efficient way of communicating data across networked devices. Because data is exchanged in small pieces, there is less chance of bottle necking.

They have different structures that are dependent upon the type of packets that is being sent.
